How it works

  • · Sources: Fed press releases, Politico, Bloomberg RSS + daily yfinance market headlines
  • · 10 most recent articles are sent to Claude Haiku in a single batch — duplicate coverage of the same event counts as one signal, not many
  • · Haiku identifies up to 5 distinct policy events, scores each −1.0 to +1.0, then recency decay (half-life 3 days) is applied
  • · Scores are scaled and summed; total is capped at ±0.15 before being added to the quantitative regime score
  • · At full cap (±0.15), policy can shift a borderline Neutral → Risk-On or Risk-Off — but cannot flip Risk-On → Risk-Off alone
  • · Quantitative signals (VIX, SPX trend, yields, copper) always contribute the larger portion of the regime score

How This Works

[ + expand ] [ − collapse ]

The system ranks every stock and ETF in the universe against each other each week. It does not predict absolute prices. It asks: which candidates rank highest on momentum, quality, and trend right now? Weights shift based on the current market regime. After 30+ weeks the IC feedback loop nudges weights toward whichever factors are actually predicting returns.

Selection Factors

Factor Default Weight Signal
Momentum 50% (Risk-On) / 20% (Risk-Off) 12M-1M return rank (Jegadeesh-Titman). Skips last month to avoid short-term reversal.
Quality 25% (Risk-On) / 50% (Risk-Off) Sharpe ratio + low max-drawdown + low volatility — ranked cross-sectionally.
Trend 25% all regimes Above 200MA + golden cross + 4-quarter return consistency.
